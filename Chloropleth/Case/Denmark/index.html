Denmark : The Flexicurity Model

Denmark, a country with both low labor index violation scores and extreme poverty scores could potentially be an exemplary model for other countries to follow. Workers feel secure in their jobs and also have room to bring up and resolve potential problems relating to atypical employment. Studies have shown the effectiveness of the famous Danish ‘flexicurity’ model, which has now been adapted by the European Commission. This model has redefined the needs of workers by deprioritizing job security. A flexible market does indeed lead to more hiring and firing but still, Danish workers enjoy better outcomes. There is no legal minimum wage but workers have higher pay than in other countries. How is all of this possible? There are two answers - a generous social security system and regular negotiations between employers and labour unions. The labor market has high levels of collective agreement coverage and union density! Check out the official website of Denmark to learn more about the flexicurity model.
