Mexico: Progressa policy to reduce child labor

Mexico is among the 15 largest economies in the world and the second largest economy in Latin America. Progressa was an innovative Mexican program that provides cash transfers to poor rural households, on condition that their children attend school and their family visits local health centers regularly. In 1998, Progresa was launched in 506 rural communities from seven states in Mexico. As a result of this program it increased school enrollment, attendance, progress through grades especially for girls. It also helped to reduce child labor by breaking the trade-off between higher consumption today and higher future consumption. The program did suffer from several issues including the quality and availability of services, political context (centralisation of education, politicization, corruption) & social norms. It also suffered from the issue of mistargeting and rising tension between neighbors that did receive the program and others that did not.
