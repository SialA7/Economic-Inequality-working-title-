Palm Oil Industry in Indonesia

Indonesia is 22nd place for labor rights violations, with most of its violations being connected to fundamental civil liberties and the right of workers to establish and join organizations. In Indonesia, most of the working poor are living at near poverty rates, rather than moderate or extreme poverty. Currently, indonesia is a lower middle-income status economy with high income inequality rates. As the world's top producer and exporter of palm oil, we dove deeper into the industry and discovered that for workers, the industry linked to the world's top beauty brands has a hidden dark side. The dark side features invisible women working on palm oil plantations. Only the fruits of their labor are visible, while their struggles remain invisible to the general public and they see no compensation for their work. Often, women work without pay to help their husbands meet otherwise impossible daily quotas.
